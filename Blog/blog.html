Arsenal FC History & Legends

Club History

Founded in 1886 as Dial Square by workers at the Royal Arsenal in Woolwich, south London. Renamed Royal Arsenal shortly afterwards and became Woolwich Arsenal in 1893.

Herbert Chapman Era

The legendary manager who revolutionized English football in the 1930s. Introduced tactics like the WM formation, floodlights, and numbered shirts. Led Arsenal to their first major trophies.

The Invincibles

The 2003-04 season saw Arsenal complete an entire Premier League campaign unbeaten (26 wins, 12 draws). Managed by Arsène Wenger, featuring legends like Henry, Vieira, and Bergkamp.

Thierry Henry

Arsenal's all-time top scorer with 228 goals. The French striker won two Premier League titles and two FA Cups with the club. Known for his incredible pace, skill, and finishing ability.

Arsenal's Legacy

With 13 league titles, a record 14 FA Cups, and two European trophies, the Gunners have a rich history of success. The club has consistently been at the forefront of English football, known for attractive, attacking football and developing world-class talent.
